Hair Care Tips

How to Prep Your Hair Before a Braid Appointment

Proper hair prep before your braid appointment helps your style last longer, look cleaner, and feel more comfortable. Here's what Yulis recommends for clients in Cutler Bay and South Miami.

Walking into a braid appointment with well-prepped hair makes a noticeable difference. Yulis sees clients every week at her Cutler Bay studio, and the ones who arrive with clean, detangled, and stretched hair consistently get styles that last longer and look sharper from day one.

1. Wash and Deep Condition 1–2 Days Before

Start with a clarifying shampoo to remove product buildup, excess oil, and environmental residue. Follow with a deep conditioner to restore moisture and strengthen your natural hair. Yulis recommends avoiding heavy leave-in conditioners or butter-based products on the day of your appointment, as they can cause slippage and make parts less defined.

2. Detangle Thoroughly

Take your time with detangling. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers, working from the ends up to the roots. If your hair is tightly coiled or heavily textured, a detangling spray or light conditioner can help reduce friction. The goal is smooth, knot-free sections that Yulis can work with efficiently.

3. Stretch or Blow-Dry Your Hair

Stretched hair is easier to section, part, and braid. You can achieve this through blow-drying on a cool setting, banding, or twist-outs. Stretched hair also gives Yulis a clearer view of your natural hairline and density, which helps her create balanced, symmetrical parts.

4. Avoid Heavy Oils and Styling Products

On the day of your appointment, skip heavy oils, gels, and edge control. These products can make your hair slippery, which affects how well the braids grip and hold. If your scalp feels dry, a light water-based moisturizer is fine — but less is more.

5. Wear Comfortable Clothing

Braid appointments can last several hours. Wear a loose, comfortable top — ideally something that's easy to take on and off if Yulis needs access to the back of your neck. Dark colors help if you're getting colored extensions, as stray fibers won't show on your shirt.

6. Eat and Stay Hydrated

Sitting for a long braid session can be tiring. Eat a light meal before your appointment and bring water. Yulis provides a comfortable environment, but being well-fed and hydrated helps you stay relaxed throughout the process.

7. Communicate Your Goals

Bring reference photos if you have a specific look in mind. Yulis will assess your hair length, density, and condition, then recommend the best braid size, style, and tension for your goals. If you're unsure, she'll guide you toward a style that complements your features and lifestyle.
